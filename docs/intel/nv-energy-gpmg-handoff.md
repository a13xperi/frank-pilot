To the NV Energy New Development Coordinator team,

Global Property Management Group (GPMG) manages a 17-building affordable/senior-housing portfolio across southern
Nevada and is validating the **existing electrical service of record** at each premise. We are evaluating spare
service capacity for a potential on-site load addition and battery storage, and need the as-built service facts.
We'd like to **open a brief service-planning consultation** for the portfolio and would appreciate one coordinator
across all 17 premises. We can provide owner-of-record authorization, account numbers, and a site contact on
request to speed your lookup.

For **each premise/meter listed below**, please confirm or advise the process to obtain:

1. **Serving transformer rating (kVA)** and configuration (pad-mount / pole / vault).
2. **Service voltage and phase** (e.g., 120/208 V 3Φ, 277/480 V 3Φ).
3. **Number of meters / services on the parcel.** Two clusters of adjacent, commonly-owned parcels may share
   service — please confirm whether each is metered separately or shares a service: **(a)** the 1327 H St campus
   (3 parcels, UT 1/2/3); **(b)** Donna Louise 1 & 2 (6225 / 6275 Donna St).
4. **Available/spare capacity** on the serving transformer, the **current peak demand of record**, and any
   **recent service-upgrade history**.

We are also scoping two potential additions per site and would appreciate the right process/contact for each:

5. **Load addition:** whether a service-planning study (Rule 9 South line-extension review) is required to add a
   firm **~180 kW behind-the-meter load** per site against the existing service, and how to initiate it (we
   understand new-load requests run through the New Construction Portal / NDC).
6. **Storage interconnection (Rule 15 South / RE-1):** the pathway to interconnect a **~250 kW / ~400–500 kWh**
   behind-the-meter battery per site. We understand standalone storage <1 MW files via **PowerClerk**
   (`nvenetmeter.powerclerk.com`, Attachment B) under the **RE-1** design standard, and we will pre-screen feeder
   **hosting capacity** on the DRP hosting-capacity map. Please confirm the current pathway and whether a
   **non-export** configuration expedites review.

Items 1–4 are the immediate need (existing-service facts). Items 5–6 are forward-looking — route them to the
right desks if that's faster. The battery holds *net* grid draw flat, so most sites may add the compute load
without a service upgrade.
