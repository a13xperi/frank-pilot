# Frank Voice — Professional Voice Clone (PVC) Recording Script & Spec

**Goal:** record ~30 minutes of clean, single-speaker audio in Frank's warm
housing-onboarding register to train a **Professional Voice Clone** that replaces the
current 8-minute Instant Voice Clone (`l5f4lE0GSzze4zyG71le`). Higher fidelity, more
consistent across the varied things Frank says on a call.

**Decisions locked (Jun 14):** Creator-tier ElevenLabs upgrade · same speaker as the
current clone · ~30 min target. **Speaker must do ElevenLabs' consent voice-captcha** at
upload (proves rights to clone the voice).

---

## 1. Recording spec (read before recording — PVC amplifies flaws)

- **One speaker only.** No second voice, no overlapping talk, no background music.
- **Quiet, treated room.** Carpet/curtains/closet beats a bare hard-walled room. No HVAC
  hum, fridge, traffic, keyboard, phone buzz. Record when the space is quietest.
- **Consistent setup the whole time:** same mic, same distance (a fist's width, slightly
  off-axis to avoid plosives), same input gain, same energy and pace. Don't move closer/
  farther between takes.
- **Mic:** any decent USB condenser or a phone with a wind screen in a quiet room beats a
  laptop mic. Use a pop filter or angle slightly off the mouth.
- **Levels:** aim ~ -12 to -6 dBFS peaks, never clipping. Leave ~1s of silence at the head
  and tail; trim long mid-takes.
- **Format:** WAV or FLAC if possible; otherwise MP3 ≥ 192 kbps, 44.1 kHz. Mono is fine.
- **Pace & tone:** warm, calm, clear — the way Frank should sound greeting a prospective
  tenant. Smile while reading the friendly lines; it carries. Natural, not "announcer."
- **Mistakes:** pause, then re-read the sentence cleanly. You can keep one file rolling and
  trim later, or stop/start per block. Multiple files are fine to upload.
- **Quantity:** ~30 min of *kept* audio. Record a bit extra (~35–40 min) so trims still
  leave 30. More is better if the speaker has the stamina.

**At upload:** create the PVC in ElevenLabs (Creator+), add these files, complete the
**voice-captcha** (the speaker reads ElevenLabs' on-screen verification phrase), then let
it train (a few hours). See GL-F5/GL-F6 for the create + hot-swap steps.

## 2. The script

Read naturally, in Frank's voice. The blocks are designed to cover the sounds, numbers,
and phrasings Frank actually uses on calls, plus enough prosodic variety (statements,
questions, warmth, reassurance) for a robust clone. Read each block; if you finish under
~30 min, re-read Blocks F and G with slight variations to top up.

### Block A — Greetings & openers (warm, varied)
> Hi there, this is Frank with Donna Louise. Thanks so much for calling — how can I help you today?
> Hello! You've reached Frank, the leasing assistant for Donna Louise. I'm really glad you called.
> Good morning! This is Frank. Are you calling about an apartment, or is there something else I can help with?
> Hey, welcome — I'm Frank. I help folks get started with their application, and I'll walk you through every step.
> Of course. I'd be happy to help you with that. Let's take it one step at a time.

### Block B — Recording-consent disclosure (Nevada two-party consent — read clearly, several phrasings)
> Before we get started, I want to let you know that this call may be recorded for quality and to keep an accurate record of your application. Is that okay with you?
> Just so you know, our conversation is being recorded so nothing gets lost. You can ask me to stop at any time.
> For your records and ours, this call is recorded. If you'd rather I not, just say the word and I'll make a note instead.
> Thank you for confirming. I appreciate it.

### Block C — Core intake questions (the real funnel)
> Can I start with your first and last name?
> And what's the best phone number to reach you at?
> Is there an email address you'd like us to use as well?
> How many people would be living in the apartment, including yourself?
> Are there any children who'd be living with you? How many, and what ages?
> Do you have a rough sense of your household's monthly or yearly income? A ballpark is fine.
> What size apartment are you looking for — a studio, a one-bedroom, or a two-bedroom?
> When are you hoping to move in? Is there a date you need to be in by?
> Are you currently on any other waiting lists, or is Donna Louise your main one?
> Do you have any pets? And are there any accessibility needs I should make a note of?
> Is there anything else about your situation you'd like me to pass along to the team?

### Block D — Empathy & reassurance (warmth, falling intonation)
> I completely understand, and I want you to know you're in the right place.
> That sounds like a lot to manage. Take your time — there's no rush on my end.
> You're doing everything right by calling in. We'll get you set up.
> I know paperwork can feel overwhelming. I'll make this as simple as I can, I promise.
> Don't worry about having every document ready this minute. We can start, and you can send the rest in.
> Congratulations — that's wonderful news. I'm happy for you.
> I'm sorry to hear that. Let's see what we can do to help.

### Block E — Numbers, money, dates, addresses, spelling (coverage)
> Your confirmation number is 4, 7, 2, 9, 1.
> The rent is one thousand, two hundred and fifty dollars a month, and the deposit is five hundred.
> That comes to two thousand, three hundred and seventy-five dollars total.
> We're open Monday through Friday, from nine in the morning until five in the afternoon, and Saturdays until one.
> Your appointment is on Tuesday, June the fifteenth, at ten forty-five.
> The property is at 1, 4, 8, 2, 5 Donna Louise Drive, Las Vegas, Nevada, 8, 9, 1, 0, 1.
> Let me read that phone number back: 7, 2, 5 — 2, 6, 7 — 2, 4, 8, 8.
> Could you spell your last name for me? I want to make sure I get it right.
> So that's P, as in Peter — E — R, as in Robert — I. Did I get that right?
> One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten. Twenty, thirty, forty, fifty, a hundred, a thousand.

### Block F — Confirmations, next steps & the app link
> Perfect — I've got all of that down. Let me read it back to make sure it's correct.
> Great. The next step is a short application, and I can text you a secure link right now if you'd like.
> I'm sending that link to your phone — it should arrive in just a moment. Tap it whenever you're ready.
> Once you finish the application, someone from our team will follow up within a couple of business days.
> Is there a good time of day for us to reach you? Mornings or afternoons?
> You're all set. Thank you so much for your time today — we're glad you reached out.
> If you think of anything later, just call this number back and ask for Frank. I'm always happy to help.

### Block G — Edge cases (questions, polite handling)
> I want to make sure I hear you correctly — could you say that one more time for me?
> It sounds like the line cut out for a second. Are you still there?
> No problem at all — would you like me to call you back at a better time?
> I'm not able to help with that one myself, but I'll make sure the right person follows up with you.
> I completely respect that. I won't keep you — have a wonderful rest of your day.
> If I miss you, I'll leave a short message and you can call us right back.

### Block H — Free-form natural passages (prosodic variety; read relaxed)
> Finding a place to live is a big deal, and it should feel that way. My whole job is to make the first step easy, so that the rest feels possible. You don't need to have it all figured out before you call — you just need to start, and I'll be here for the rest of it.
> Every person I talk to has a different story, and that's exactly how it should be. Some folks know precisely what they want; others are just beginning to look. Either way, you're welcome here, and there's no wrong question to ask me.

### Block I — Phonetic coverage (read once, crisply)
> The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
> Pack my box with five dozen liquor jugs.
> She sells seashells by the seashore on a sunny Thursday afternoon.
> How much wood would a woodchuck chuck if a woodchuck could chuck wood?
> Bright blue balloons, gentle green gardens, warm orange evenings, and quiet violet nights.
